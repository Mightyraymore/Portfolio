Why Software Development?

I began professionally as a black-box tester at Citibank in 2012. It was a great feeling to be a part of team. The most fulfilling aspect of working in a team, was finding a solution to challenging problems. It’s always, helpful working with others and bouncing ideas off of one another. Sometimes that is not possible and that is when sniffing around on the Web is great also.

It was a privilege emerging myself into learning about credit card processes in the scope of banking. One of the most fascinating things about software development, is planning how the software should behave and how all the different classes of code interact with different objects. It takes a lot of planning, to bring together a hundred different people, to play different roles in such projects.

When it comes to software, it is constantly changing and you never just become rooted to one thing. Perhaps this is the very reason I feel compelled to be in this profession, because of all the new things that I learn on my journeys. Along the way I have been inspired by different people, weather they are project managers, developers, testers, or stakeholders. These are the reasons I love what I do and continue in this arena.

Why Software Testing?

Software testing is crucial to the success of meeting the needs and requirements of the consumer who uses it. Without testing, I could just imagine the scenarios where the development team could be embarrassed with broken software especially when hours, days, weeks and even months of work come to a grinding halt. An example, if code written 2 months down the road and is causing unexpected things to happen to other aspects of the software. All sorts of issues would likely occur. There would be serious risks and consequences to the budget and schedule of that project.

It’s fun to learn about what the consumer expects, and learn about their needs. The responsibility to create test case scenarios is interesting, because the challenge of finding creative ways to test against those rules can be broad. It’s also my job to break the rules, or at least try. How many jobs or careers can people have that luxury, after all?

My Education

At Colorado Technical University, I chose to begin my studies into software development. In 2010, I began to earn my bachelor’s degree in software engineering. The way I viewed taking on this challenge, was to conquer my fears of stepping into a domain I had never stepped in. I had the some of the best times pursuing this goal, in technologies in software engineering

This was not a brick and mortar school, this was all online. We had challenges working together on group assignments, which led to not only learning how to do projects as a team, but how to effectively organize and work cohesively as one unit. Our courses were project based, so we had to research and read a variety of books to implement our different projects. It was very heavy hands on learning, which is the best way I learn.
